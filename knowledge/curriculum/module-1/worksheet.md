## Build your Project

Open the Google Doc (link on page 1 or scan the QR below). Find your name. Your starter Project is underneath it.

1. Open claude.ai and sign in
2. Click **Projects** in the left sidebar
3. Click **New Project** — name it anything
4. Find **your name** in the shared Google Doc
5. Read the "Why this works" notes underneath your starter
6. Copy the instructions block
7. Paste into your Project's **Custom Instructions** field
8. Personalize: swap the generic parts for your real situation
9. (Optional) Upload a real file, or paste a paragraph of context
10. Type your first real task and hit send
11. Read the output. It will be imperfect. That's the point.

---

### If you're stuck

- **Output too generic?** Add more specific context about your situation.
- **Claude made something up?** Add a constraint: "Flag anything you're uncertain about. Say 'I don't know' when that's accurate."
- **Not sure what to type?** Start with: "Here is [your task]. Do it using the instructions I gave you."

### If you finish early

Run a second prompt on a different task. Same Project, different question. See what happens.

## What makes a Project work

The starter you just used has six components. They all do.

| Component | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Who Claude is acting as — specific expertise, not "helpful assistant" |
| **Context** | Your situation: what the output is for, why you need it |
| **Inputs** | What you'll provide each time (structured, named) |
| **Criteria** | What good output looks like (numbered, specific, verifiable) |
| **Constraints** | What Claude should not do (hallucination guards, tone, scope) |
| **Verification** | How you check the output before acting on it |
