# Competitive Landscape Learnings

## Context
Comprehensive competitive analysis conducted April 2026, covering 25+ competitors across Chicago and nationally.

## Key Findings

### 1. Unlock Intelligence occupies a genuine white space
No competitor offers the exact combination of: 8-hour live format + non-technical audience + 3 tangible deliverables + university instructor credential + mid-market pricing.

### 2. Competitors fall into three buckets
- **Too shallow** (90 min - 4 hrs): ExperiencePoint, GA workshops, Certstaffix — introduce concepts but don't produce lasting behavioral change
- **Too long** (6-21 weeks): UChicago ($2K-$4.5K), Kellogg ($3.2K), MIT ($9K), Columbia ($5K) — time commitment busy teams won't make; 5-15% completion rates
- **Too expensive** for small teams: Marketing AI Institute ($75K+/half-day), McKinsey/BCG ($100K-$500K+)

### 3. Top threat competitors
- **Teamland (AI First)** — Closest format match with Chicago-specific SEO pages and dedicated city landing pages. Workshops are shorter (half-day) with fewer deliverables. Primary trust signal: "since 2017"
- **General Assembly** — Strong brand, 32-hour AI program + $500 role workshops. Career-change oriented, less corporate-training focused. Free 1-hour intro class as lead magnet
- **UChicago/Kellogg** — Brand prestige competition but 6-21 week commitment is their weakness
- **Revenue AI** — $1,000/person for similar format (5+ min), closest pricing comp but no university credential or Chicago focus

### 4. Self-paced platforms are the biggest indirect competitor
Coursera ($399/yr), LinkedIn Learning ($380/yr), Udemy ($360/yr) — most enterprises already pay for these. Their 5-15% completion rates are our strongest argument.

### 5. Google uses "AI fluent"
Google's "Grow with Google" program uses the exact phrase "AI fluent" — validates term resonance but means we need to differentiate our branded use (free generic basics vs. customized role-specific transformation).

### 6. No one owns Chicago AI training
Teamland has a Chicago landing page but is a national brand. No competitor has built a Chicago-native AI training brand for non-technical professionals.

## Open Questions
- How quickly will OpenAI Academy (free) commoditize foundational AI literacy?
- When will GA or Coursera add live cohort programs that directly compete?
- Is there room for a certification play (no dominant cert exists for "AI fluency for non-technical teams")?
