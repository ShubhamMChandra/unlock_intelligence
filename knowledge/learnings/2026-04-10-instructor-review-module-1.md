# 2026-04-10 — Instructor agent review of Module 1 run-of-show

**Context:** Two days before the 2026-04-12 beta. Run-of-show was reviewed by an instructor agent after two rounds of persona testing. This review focuses on what synthetic personas can't catch — room logistics, physical space, energy management, facilitation craft, and real-time stall patterns.

---

## Pedagogical gaps

**Matrix block is cognitively overloaded for its position.** The demo creates emotional energy; the matrix requires analytical energy. That's a gear-shift with no transition mechanic. Fix: print the matrix visual, hand it out before Block 3. Physical artifacts reduce working memory load and become a take-home reference.

**"Before AI / After AI" row labels are weaker than they should be.** They imply simple replacement. The horizontal thesis is about redesigning the entire row. Consider "Today" and "Rebuilt" instead.

**No comprehension check between matrix and build.** Add 30 seconds: "Before we build — someone tell me the difference between vertical and horizontal thinking." Point at someone specific. If they stumble, you need 60 more seconds before keyboards open.

---

## Pacing corrections

- **Block 7 (closer) budgeted at 7 min — will take 10.** Hard constraint should be "start Block 7 with 15 min left," not 10.
- **Block 2 (demo) could hit 18-20 min** if volunteer reacts strongly. Have a redirect: "Hold that thought — that's exactly what you're about to build."
- **Block 3 (matrix):** make it interactive. Ask the room to fill in cells instead of monologuing the mapping.
- **Show-and-tell dead air risk:** cable-switching between 5 people kills energy. Have people AirDrop screenshots to instructor's laptop during build phase. Instructor projects them.

---

## Energy management

Two valleys to manage:

1. **Minutes 20-28 (matrix block).** Dopamine crash after demo. Don't lecture — ask the room to fill in cells with you.
2. **Minutes 35-55 (build phase).** Longest single block. Add a midpoint check at ~15 min in: "Quick check — who's gotten their first output? Thumbs up." 20 seconds, reminds the room they're in a shared experience.

---

## Build phase stall patterns (expect all four)

| Stall | Archetype | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| The reader | Reads everything before acting (lawyers) | Walk over at min 5: "Paste it first, run it once, then edit." |
| The improviser | Rewrites starter from scratch (founders) | Walk over at min 7: "What are you changing?" If cosmetic: "Run it first." |
| The perfectionist | Wants to iterate before showing | At min 20 to room: "If you've gotten one output, you're done for today." |
| The frozen | Stares at screen, blank-page paralysis | Walk over and physically paste the starter for them while narrating. |

**Pairing trick:** Seat self-sufficient people next to each other so they troubleshoot together. Frees instructor for the hard cases first.

## Facilitation craft — specific lines

### Strong (keep)
- "Watch what it DOES, not just what it says." — emphasis on DOES with a pause before it.
- "Would you actually use this on Monday?" — behavioral, not evaluative.

### Adjust
- **30-sec theory beat:** Move from Phase B (mid-run) to Phase C (after output). Don't interrupt the thing creating the emotional reaction. Proof first, then explanation.
- **"I'll help anyone stalled in 15 minutes after we wrap":** Reframe as abundance: "We have 5 more minutes here, and I'm sticking around after for anyone who wants to keep going."
- **Closer bridge:** Current "this is Hour 4" isn't enough. Use: "The distance between one step and six steps is not talent, and it's not code. It's the next three sessions. I'm showing you the destination, not showing off."
- **Cut "questions later"** from end of closer. It implies a Q&A that doesn't exist. End on the strong line about chaining steps and pivot straight to debrief.

## Deliberate failure moment

Too vague for a first run. **Pre-build the failure.** Before Sunday, find a prompt that reliably produces a fabrication (a specific statistic, a case citation in cannabis regulatory space, a filing deadline). Have it in a tab. If no natural failure by minute 15 of build phase, walk to projector and trigger it yourself. After 5+ runs, you can let failures happen naturally. On the first run, script it.

## Missing pieces to add

1. **Comprehension check** (30 sec between matrix and build)
2. **Fast-finisher stretch challenge:** "If you're done, try a second prompt on a different task. Same Project, different question."
3. **Cadence beat** (30 sec at end of closer): "Run it once this week on a real task. Next week, update the instructions. That's the loop — build, run, tighten."
4. **Physical matrix printout** — hand out before Block 3
5. **Food and coffee out before people arrive.** Don't put food near the projector.
6. **Script the opening 15 seconds.** Don't wing the first two sentences.
7. **Test 2 simultaneous Claude sessions on venue wifi Saturday.**

---

## Show-and-tell ordering

Don't go around the table. Pick the order based on energy during the build:
- Start with the most excited person (sets the bar)
- End with the most surprising cross-industry output
- Have a bridge for anyone who didn't finish: "Show us the instructions you wrote, even without the output. The instructions are the tool."
