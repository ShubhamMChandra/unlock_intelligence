# ICP & Buyer Personas

## Primary Buyer: The HR Champion

**Who they are:** HR professional (L&D Director, HR Manager, VP of People) at a mid-to-large company who is responsible for workforce development. They've been asked — or have decided on their own — to figure out AI training for their team.

**How they buy:** Champion-led B2B. They discover the program through LinkedIn, a referral, or direct outreach from Shubham. They evaluate it. If convinced, they advocate internally — presenting to their VP or CFO for budget approval. Some attend themselves first to validate, then buy seats for their team.

**What they need to see on the site:**
- Organizational capability framing — "your company needs this," not "you need this"
- Instructor credibility they can verify (UChicago, Digital Realty)
- Tangible deliverables their team gets (prompt playbook, workflow map, 90-day strategy)
- Risk reversal (refund guarantee)
- Something they can forward to their VP without embarrassment

**The one-sentence pitch:**
"A live, instructor-led program that makes your team AI-fluent in 8 hours — led by a University of Chicago instructor."

## How They Find Us (Pre-Launch)

1. **Direct outreach** — Shubham's network (Digital Realty, UChicago, former NJOY/Garner colleagues)
2. **LinkedIn** — Shubham's content about AI for non-technical professionals
3. **Referral** — A colleague who attended or heard about the program
4. **Cold inbound** — Unlikely at pre-launch, but possible from LinkedIn content

## What They Need at Each Stage

| Stage | What They're Thinking | What They Need |
|-------|----------------------|----------------|
| Awareness | "Interesting, but is this legit?" | Instructor credentials, clear program description |
| Evaluation | "Is this worth my team's time?" | Curriculum detail, deliverables, time commitment |
| Justification | "How do I sell this internally?" | ROI framing, forwarding-safe copy, proposal they can share |
| Decision | "What's the risk?" | Refund guarantee, founding cohort pricing, clear process |

## Key Objections

| Objection | How We Address It |
|-----------|------------------|
| "Is 8 hours enough?" | Deliverables prove substance. Framing: "Your team is applying skills by Monday." |
| "Who else has done this?" | Pre-launch: instructor credentials. Post-launch: testimonials. |
| "What's the ROI?" | Prompt playbook + workflow map = measurable time savings. Executive summary included. |
| "How is this different from Coursera?" | Live, cohort-based, deliverable-focused, 8 hours not 8 weeks, instructor-led not video |
| "This looks like every other AI course" | It's not a course — it's a training program with tangible outputs. Show the deliverables. |
| "The team is only 2 people" | UChicago and Digital Realty credentials establish authority. Small = direct instructor access. |

## What Kills the Deal

- Startup-bro language ("dangerous", "built different", "disrupt")
- Fake social proof ("Cohort 5" with zero alumni)
- Hidden pricing with no context (they need budget range for internal approval)
- A site that speaks to individuals instead of teams
- Anything they'd be embarrassed to forward to their boss
