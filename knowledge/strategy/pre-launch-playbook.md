# Pre-Launch Playbook: 0 to First 10 Customers

## The Model: Champion-Led B2B

We don't sell directly to procurement. We find HR professionals, convince them the program is worth championing internally, and they pull us into their company. Some attend first, some buy seats directly.

---

## Phase 1: Warm Network (Customers 1-3)

**Timeline:** Now — first 4 weeks

**How it works:**
First customers will NOT come from the website. They'll come from Shubham's professional network. The site's job at this phase is to be a credible "leave-behind" — something Shubham sends after a personal conversation that doesn't embarrass him.

**Actions:**
1. Shubham reaches out to 20-30 contacts at companies where he has relationships:
   - Digital Realty colleagues
   - UChicago network (faculty, alumni, students' employers)
   - Former NJOY and Garner Health colleagues
2. The pitch: "I'm launching a professional AI training program. I'm looking for 3-5 companies to run the founding cohort with at a significant discount in exchange for honest feedback and a testimonial."
3. Follow up with the site link + a personalized note

**What the site needs:** Clean design, honest framing, instructor credentials front and center. No fake proof.

**Success metric:** 3 companies commit to the founding cohort.

---

## Phase 2: LinkedIn as Distribution (Customers 4-7)

**Timeline:** Weeks 2-8 (overlaps with Phase 1)

**How it works:**
Shubham has the credential to post authoritatively about AI on LinkedIn. Build an audience of HR professionals and L&D leaders through consistent content.

**Content strategy:**
- Post 2-3x per week
- Topics that demonstrate expertise without selling:
  - "5 prompting mistakes I see executives make"
  - "How I automated [specific process] at Digital Realty"
  - "What I teach my UChicago students about AI that surprises them"
  - "The difference between AI-curious and AI-competent teams"
- Every 5th post can mention the program directly
- LinkedIn profile should link to the site

**Lead capture:**
- Create a downloadable curriculum PDF (email-gated)
- This becomes the bridge between "interesting post" and "I should look into this program"

**What the site needs:** Lead capture (email for curriculum PDF), low friction, clear value prop.

**Success metric:** Consistent inbound inquiries from LinkedIn. 2-4 companies sign up.

---

## Phase 3: Outbound to L&D (Customers 8-10)

**Timeline:** After founding cohort completes

**How it works:**
Now you have real assets: testimonials, a refined curriculum, proof the program runs and delivers. Time for targeted outbound to L&D directors and HR VPs.

**Actions:**
1. Get video testimonials from 3 founding cohort participants (focused on team outcomes)
2. Write a case study from the best founding cohort experience
3. Update the site with testimonials and case study
4. Begin targeted outreach:
   - LinkedIn InMail to L&D directors at target companies
   - Email outreach with case study attached
   - Referrals from founding cohort participants

**What the site needs:** Testimonials, case study, clear pricing, easy enrollment path.

**Success metric:** Enterprise deals closing from outbound. 3+ companies.

---

## What the Site's Job Is at Each Phase

| Phase | Site's Job | Critical Element |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| 1 (Warm) | Credible leave-behind after personal outreach | Clean design, honest framing, instructor credentials |
| 2 (LinkedIn) | Convert inbound traffic from content | Lead capture, low friction, curriculum download |
| 3 (Outbound) | Close deals that start in email/call | Testimonials, case study, pricing, easy enrollment |

## Key Principle

**Don't optimize the site for Phase 3 when you're in Phase 1.** The site doesn't need enterprise procurement features, ROI calculators, or self-service checkout yet. It needs to look credible when Shubham texts a link to a former colleague.
