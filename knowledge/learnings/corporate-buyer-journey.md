# Corporate Buyer Journey Insights

## Context
Research from B2B procurement studies, Training Industry, Deloitte, and Josh Bersin reports (2026).

## Key Findings

### 1. The 10-20 week decision cycle

| Stage | What Happens | Duration |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Problem Recognition | L&D leader notices AI skills gap | Ongoing |
| Internal Championing | A "champion" builds the case internally | 2-6 weeks |
| Research & Shortlisting | Average 22 working hours across stakeholders | 2-4 weeks |
| Vendor Evaluation | 3-5 vendors; website, social proof, testimonials reviewed | 2-3 weeks |
| Proposal & Approval | Budget approval; CFO sign-off for >$10K | 2-4 weeks |
| Scheduling & Delivery | Program scheduling, logistics | 2-4 weeks |

### 2. The website must be a "sales assistant"
The internal champion uses the website to sell upward. Every section should generate ammunition for the champion's internal pitch — downloadable PDFs, ROI data, credibility proof.

### 3. What the champion needs
- Downloadable Program Overview PDF (leave-behind for stakeholder meetings)
- Clear pricing or at least a range (71% of B2B buyers rate price transparency as "very important")
- ROI framing they can copy into their business case
- Credibility proof (UChicago, industry stats)
- Risk mitigation (money-back guarantee)
- Speed assurance ("Team trained in one week, not one quarter")

### 4. Listed pricing reduces sales cycles by 37%
But enterprise/team pricing should remain proposal-based. Hybrid approach: list individual price, anchor team pricing with "typically 15-30% below individual rates."

### 5. Buyer-focused questions differ from participant questions
L&D buyers ask: "How do you measure outcomes?" "What does the executive summary include?" "How does scheduling work for distributed teams?" These are different from participant FAQs about technical prerequisites or session format.

### 6. Corporate procurement is multi-stakeholder
The champion is rarely the final decision-maker. Budget authority increasingly centralized with CFO scrutiny on training ROI. The executive summary deliverable is critical because it gives the buyer proof the investment worked.
