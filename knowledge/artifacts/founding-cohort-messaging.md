## Rejected Copy -- What We Changed and Why

Reference this table before writing new copy. These patterns keep recurring.

| Rejected Copy | Why It Was Rejected | What Replaced It |
|---|---|---|
| "Become the AI Expert Your Company Didn't Know It Needed" | Speaks to individual career anxiety, not the team buyer who is paying | "Make Your Team AI-Fluent in 8 Hours." |
| "Cohort 5 -- Now Enrolling" | Dishonest. Zero customers. HR buyers who Google us find nothing to corroborate it. | "Founding Cohort -- Spring 2026" |
| "become dangerous with the tools engineers build" | "Dangerous" is startup-bro language. HR departments are risk-averse by function. | "confident, competent users" |
| "Built Different. On Purpose." | TikTok meme phrase. Reads as immature for enterprise training buyers. | "Why This Program Works" |
| "Eight Hours to Authority" | "Authority" is individual aspiration language, wrong audience for team training. | "Two Sessions. Real Deliverables." |
| "Reserve Your Seat" | Trust violation. No reservation happens -- it opens a contact form. | "Get a Custom Proposal" |
| "Your AI Authority Profile" | Sounds like a LinkedIn feature, not a training outcome. | "What Your Team Leaves With" |
| "Join the Next Cohort" | Implies previous cohorts exist. They do not. | "Get a Custom Proposal" |
| "Investment details shared directly" | Reads evasive. HR buyers need budget context before engaging. | Founding cohort framing with proposal language |
| "Organisation" (British spelling) | US-targeted product. US buyers notice British spelling as off-brand. | "Organization" |

## Usage Rules

1. **Do not invent previous cohorts.** We are pre-launch. "Founding cohort" is the only honest framing.
2. **Do not mix audiences.** The primary buyer is the HR/L&D decision-maker. Individual enrollment exists but is secondary.
3. **Do not use aspirational language.** "Competence" over "authority". "Capability" over "mastery". "Build" over "transform".
4. **Every CTA must describe what actually happens.** If it opens a form, say so. If it scrolls to a section, say so.
5. **US English everywhere.** Organization, customize, enrollment.
