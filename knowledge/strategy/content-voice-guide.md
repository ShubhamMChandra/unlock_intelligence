# Content Voice Guide

## Context
Established April 2026 during the /insights section build. This guide applies to all future content: articles, LinkedIn posts, case studies, and marketing copy.

## The Voice

**Observation-first, data-supported.** Lead with what Shubham sees from his dual vantage point (UChicago classroom + Digital Realty enterprise). Stats validate the observation — they don't replace it.

## Rules

### Do
- Lead with a specific observation, not a statistic
- Name real products: LinkedIn Learning, Coursera, ChatGPT, Custom GPTs
- Use varied sentence rhythm (short declarative + longer analytical)
- Use "I've seen" / "In my experience" sparingly — 2-3 times per article max
- Apply the VP forwarding test: would an HR Director email this to their CFO?
- End with ONE brand mention (CTA only)
- Make the reader think "huh, I hadn't thought of it that way"

### Don't
- Start any sentence with "In today's rapidly evolving landscape"
- Use "It's no secret that", "Let's dive in", "As we all know"
- Use emoji or exclamation marks
- Open paragraphs with rhetorical questions
- Use startup-bro language ("dangerous", "built different", "disrupt")
- Use generic phrases: "popular platforms", "AI tools", "many companies"
- Sound like a consultant billing by the hour

## Tone Spectrum
Professional but warm — like a smart colleague explaining something at a working dinner, not a keynote speaker on stage.

## The Unique Angles
These are Shubham's proprietary observations that competitors cannot replicate:
1. The crossover perspective (classroom + enterprise = same mistakes)
2. Building > prompting (the real unlock)
3. The 3-week adoption death pattern
4. "What do you hate?" as Socratic training opener
5. Mental model problem, not knowledge problem
