# Website Conversion Insights

## Context
Gathered from competitive website analysis and B2B conversion research, April 2026.

## Key Data Points

### Pricing & Conversion
- Cohort courses with listed prices convert **2-5x better** than "contact for pricing"
- Listed pricing reduces sales cycle by up to **37%**
- **71%** of B2B buyers rate price transparency as "very important"
- No strikethrough "regular" prices for B2B — reads as cheap

### Social Proof
- Video testimonials increase conversion by **80%**
- **93%** of B2B buyers say reviews impact purchase decisions
- Client logo bars are the fastest social proof element
- Pre-launch: instructor credentials + industry stats serve as proxy proof

### Content & Lead Generation
- **97%** of B2B marketers say thought leadership is critical
- Original research is **67% more valuable** for credibility than AI-generated content
- B2B drip campaigns see **80% higher** open rates than single-send
- Leads through drip sequences produce **47% more** sales-ready buyers

### Top Lead Magnets (ranked by expected impact)
1. AI Readiness Assessment Quiz (very high conversion, medium effort)
2. ROI Calculator — no competitor has one (very high, medium-high effort)
3. "Business Case for AI Training" template (high, low effort)
4. Free 30-min "AI Audit" consultation (high, low effort)
5. Sample AI Integration Blueprint (medium, low effort)

### Competitive Content Gaps
- Zero blog content = biggest competitive disadvantage (every competitor has a content engine)
- Teamland has city-specific SEO pages — we should replicate for Chicago
- No competitor owns "8-hour AI training" as a keyword
- Harvard program brochure download as lead capture is the model to follow
- "AI training for [specific role]" content is underserved across the market

### Best-Converting CTAs in This Space
1. "Get a free consultation" (Teamland, Improving)
2. "Download the brochure/syllabus" — lead capture without sales commitment (Harvard, GA)
3. "Enroll now" with price shown (Maven, Coursera)
4. "Request a demo" (enterprise platforms)
5. "Start free" / "Try free workshop" (GA, Google)
