# Copy Review Checklist

Run this checklist on any marketing copy before publishing -- website pages, emails, LinkedIn posts, landing pages. Print it, tape it to the wall.

---

## Voice Consistency

- [ ] **Who is the copy talking to?** HR buyer? Individual learner? Both? Pick ONE primary audience per page. Mixing audiences dilutes the message for both.
- [ ] **Search for "you" / "your"** -- does it consistently address the buyer (the person paying) or the end user (the person learning)?
- [ ] **Search for "your team" vs. "your role" / "your job"** -- these signal different audiences. "Your team" = HR/L&D buyer. "Your role" = individual professional. Don't toggle between them.

---

## CTA Audit

- [ ] **Does every CTA honestly describe what happens when you click?** "Reserve Your Seat" when there is no reservation system is a trust violation. If it opens a contact form, say "Get a Custom Proposal."
- [ ] **Are CTAs consistent across the page?** Don't use 5 different phrasings for the same contact form. Pick one primary CTA and repeat it.
- [ ] **Does the CTA name the value, not just the action?** "Get Your Custom Proposal" > "Contact Us". "See the Curriculum" > "Learn More".

---

## Tone Check

- [ ] **Read every heading aloud.** Would a real person say this in a meeting? If not, rewrite it.
- [ ] **The HR buyer forwarding test.** Can an HR professional copy-paste this into an email to their VP without editing it? If they'd feel embarrassed forwarding it, the tone is wrong.
- [ ] **Flag startup-bro language.** Red flags: "dangerous", "built different", "disrupt", "10x", "game-changer", "unlock your potential", "level up". These signal immaturity to enterprise buyers.
- [ ] **Flag defensive language.** Sections explaining who the product is NOT for, or what it is NOT, sound insecure. Cut them. Show what it IS through specifics.

---

## Specificity

- [ ] **Replace vague claims with specific ones.** "Limited seats" -> "Limited to 10 teams". "Experienced instructor" -> "University of Chicago instructor". Vague = unverifiable = ignored.
- [ ] **Replace aspirational words with outcome words.** "Authority" -> "Capability". "Mastery" -> "Competence". "Transform" -> "Build". Aspirational language over-promises; outcome language sets expectations correctly.
- [ ] **Every deliverable should be named, not described.** "An AI Integration Blueprint for your team's workflows" -- not "practical tools and resources". Names are concrete; descriptions are forgettable.

---

## Consistency

- [ ] **US vs. UK English.** Pick one. Use it everywhere. We use US English: "Organization" not "Organisation", "customize" not "customise".
- [ ] **Brand terms.** Consistent capitalization and phrasing throughout. "Founding Cohort" (capitalized) or "founding cohort" (lowercase) -- pick one and grep for violations.
- [ ] **Cohort references.** Do not reference previous cohorts that do not exist. "Founding Cohort -- Spring 2026" is honest. "Join the Next Cohort" or "Cohort 5" implies a history we don't have.

---

## Credibility

- [ ] **Don't claim what you can't prove.** No "hundreds of professionals trained" at pre-launch. No "proven results" without data. HR buyers will Google you. If they find nothing backing a claim, you lose trust permanently.
- [ ] **Lead with verifiable credentials.** University of Chicago, Digital Realty, Garner Health -- things people can look up. Put these above the fold, not buried in a bio section.
- [ ] **Refund guarantee should be visible, not buried.** Enterprise buyers see a guarantee as confidence. Hiding it looks like you're hoping they won't find it.

---

## Quick Reference: Common Mistakes We've Made

| Mistake | Example | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Mixed audience | Hero says "your team", pricing says "your career" | Pick the buyer. We chose HR/L&D. |
| Dishonest CTA | "Reserve Your Seat" for a contact form | "Get a Custom Proposal" |
| Startup-bro tone | "become dangerous with AI" | "become confident, competent users" |
| Unverifiable claim | "Cohort 5 -- Now Enrolling" | "Founding Cohort -- Spring 2026" |
| British spelling | "Organisation" | "Organization" |
| Vague deliverable | "practical tools" | "AI Integration Blueprint for your workflows" |
